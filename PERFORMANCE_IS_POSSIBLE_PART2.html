I ISSUE — safe, active… and poor

The bot was trading ~16 times a day, holding +6.6% days inside it, never once touching the floor — and netting zero. On the five RICHEST days of the whole set (7–14% of range on the table) it did exactly +0.00%. And on its good days it kept banking +2.9% on a 3.0% target. Over and over. A hair under. Never over.

A miss that consistent is not bad luck — it's a LAW acting on the result. The issue was to find which law.

R RULE — our own rule, betrayed by one term

The win-lock's written rule (R3#7): "the stand-down flatten still realizes ≥ goal." Good rule. The code set the lock at bare goal — so the lock paid the exit fee after locking, and banked goal − fee. The fix keeps the rule's own promise:

BEFORE
target +3.0%
banked +2.9%…
lock = max(lock, goal, trail)            # the flaw
AFTER — fixed in BOTH engines
target +3.0%
banks +3.0%+ ✓
lock = max(lock, goal + fee, trail)      # the rule, kept

Nothing loosened. No risk added. Note what the flaw did to LEARNING: the scoreboard paid zero for every disciplined win, thousands of times. The bot's stillness on rich days wasn't cowardice — it was obedience to a broken scoreboard. Two companion rules joined it: the Ladder Rule (master one day, then five, then all) and Monty's Serial Rule (every frozen result carries its own SHA-256 serial — a claim either has a serial or it isn't a claim).
